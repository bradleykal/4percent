How The 4% Project Helps

Not another fundraising org. We're neutral data infrastructure — the policy intelligence layer the pediatric cancer ecosystem is missing.

A Different Kind of Organization

The 4% Project is a Public Benefit Corporation — not a charity, not a lobbying firm. We exist in the gap between raw research and real policy change, providing the data infrastructure and legislative intelligence that helps the entire pediatric cancer ecosystem work more effectively.

Backed by Kingdom of Impact, a mission-driven Donor Advised Fund, through a Program-Related Investment structure. We complement — never compete with — the outstanding organizations already fighting for children.

Year 1: Discovery Before Technology

The highest-leverage strategy for a new entrant in a trust-based field is to listen first, build second.

Track 1

Academic & Clinical Institutions

  • MUSC, Dana-Farber, Texas Children's, St. Jude
  • Fellowship pipeline & workforce gaps
  • Research funding allocation patterns
  • Clinical trial access disparities
Track 2

Pharmaceutical Industry

  • PRV program utilization & reform
  • RACE Act compliance tracking
  • Pediatric trial design gaps
  • Drug pipeline mapping
Track 3

Advocacy Organizations

  • St. Baldrick's, Children's Cancer Cause
  • Kids v. Cancer, Alex's Lemonade Stand
  • Legislative intelligence gaps
  • Data infrastructure needs
Q1
Ecosystem Mapping

Identify key stakeholders, organizations, and current data gaps across all three tracks.

Q2
Deep Listening

Conduct structured interviews and workshops to understand unmet needs and pain points.

Q3
Opportunity Validation

Test and validate the highest-impact data products with early partners before building.

Q4
Year 2 Strategy

Formalize partnerships and define the technology roadmap based on validated findings.

Year 1 generates no revenue by design. The discovery phase is the investment. Understanding ecosystem needs before committing to products is how we avoid building the wrong thing — and how we earn the trust of a field that has seen too many well-intentioned entrants fail to deliver lasting impact.
