The Perverse Incentive

A brilliant young doctor completing pediatric residency faces a stark choice: specialize in pediatric oncology for 3 more years of training at ~$65K salary, then earn $175K–$304K for life — or switch to adult oncology for similar training and earn $100K–$200K more per year. Over a 30-year career, that's $3–6 million in lost lifetime earnings. We are structurally discouraging the doctors our kids need most.

Closing the pay gap requires systemic change: fair Medicare reimbursement rates for pediatric oncology, loan forgiveness programs for pediatric specialists, and NCI funding parity.
